Caenorhabditis brenneri (formerly sp. 4) is a gonochoristic species belonging to the Elegans supergroup. The sequenced strain, PB2801, is an inbred strain generated a strain (LKC28) isolated in Costa Rica [1]. See below for more information.

Species and strain

C. brenneri (sp. 4) was formally described in [1]. C. doughertyi, C. tropicalis and C. wallacei are the most closely related species [2,3].

CGC strain entry: PS1010

Raw data

The raw data can be downloaded from either ENA or NCBI:

https://www.ebi.ac.uk/ena/data/view/PRJNA51225

https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/bioproject/PRJNA51225

Assembly

The genome of C. angaria PS1010 was assembled by Ali Mortazavi and colleagues. See [2] for more details.

The genome has been resequenced using PacBio and a new assembly is underway.
