From [1]:

"Heterorhabditis bacteriophora is an entomopathogenic nematode (EPN) mutually associated with the enteric bacterium, Photorhabdus luminescens, used globally for the biological control of insects. Much of the previous research concerning H. bacteriophora has dealt with applied aspects related to biological control. However, H. bacteriophora is an excellent model to investigate fundamental processes such as parasitism and mutualism in addition to its comparative value to Caenorhabditis elegans."

See below for more information.

Assembly

The genome of H. bacteriophora M31a was sequenced, assembled and published by Xiaodong Bai and colleagues [2]. Some small, contaminant contigs have since been removed from the assembly.

Annotation

Flo McLean (Blaxter lab) repredicted genes using BRAKER [3], which utilises RNA-seq information. This new gene set appears to be a significant improvement on the original set. See parasite.wormbase.org for the original gene set.
