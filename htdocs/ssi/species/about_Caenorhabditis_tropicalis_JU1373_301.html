Caenorhabditis tropicalis (formerly sp. 11) is a androdiecious species belonging to the Elegans supergroup. The sequenced strain, JU1373, was isolated by Marie-Anne Felix from rotting torch ginger (Etlingeria elatior) flowers in La Reunion. The genome has been resequenced and is currently being reassembled. See below for more information.

Species and strain

C. tropicalis (sp. 11) was formally described in [1]. Discussion about morphological characters can be found in [2] (termed sp. 7 here). C. imperialis is the most closely related species [1].
