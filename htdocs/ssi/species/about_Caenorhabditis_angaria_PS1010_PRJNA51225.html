Caenorhabditis angaria (formerly sp. 3) is a gonochoristic species belonging to the Drosophilae supergroup. The sequenced strain, PS1010, was isolated from the abdomen of a West Indian sugercane weevel, Metamasiuis hemipterus, in Dale County, Florida [1]. The genome has been resequenced and is currently being reassembled. See below for more information.

Species and strain

C. angaria (sp. 3) was formally described in [1]. C. castelli (sp. 12) is the most closely related species [2].

CGC strain entry: PS1010

Raw data

The raw data can be downloaded from either ENA or NCBI:

https://www.ebi.ac.uk/ena/data/view/PRJNA51225

Assembly

The genome of C. angaria PS1010 was assembled by Ali Mortazavi and colleagues. See [2] for more details.

The genome has been resequenced using PacBio and a new assembly is underway.
